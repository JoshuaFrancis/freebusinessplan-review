Review criteria

Six dimensions. One structured critique. No platitudes.

01

Executive Summary

Does it answer the lender's or investor's core question within two pages? We evaluate clarity, completeness, and whether the opportunity is compellingly framed before they read any further.

02

Financial Model

Three-year projections, cash flow, and debt service coverage reviewed line by line. We flag unrealistic assumptions, missing schedules, and formatting issues that trigger lender red flags before they do.

03

Market Analysis

Is the addressable market calculation credible? Is competition acknowledged honestly? A market section that reads like a sales pitch rather than an analysis undermines the entire document.

04

Management Team

Lenders and investors fund people first, plans second. We review whether the management narrative establishes relevant experience, addresses gaps, and builds the case that this team can execute.

05

Risk Assessment

An honest risk section strengthens a plan; a missing one destroys credibility. We review whether risks are identified with specificity and whether the mitigation strategies are realistic and funded.

06

Lender Alignment

CSBFP, BDC, chartered bank, or immigration program — each has distinct formatting expectations. We check whether the document addresses the specific requirements of the target audience, not just a generic template.
